Latte Art Tutorial

by Brian Zacahua

What you will need:

  • An espresso machine with a steam wand
  • Milk pitcher
  • Ground coffee for espresso
  • Milk of choice
  • Coffee cup
Step 1:
Pull an espresso shot with your espresso grounds.
Step 2:
Pour the espresso shot into your cup.
Step 3:
Fill your pitcher with milk just below the spout.
Step 4:
Submerge half the tip of the steam wand into the milk, tilt towards one side on the pitcher, and steam.
Step 5:
With your free hand, feel the side of the pitcher, and stop steaming when the pitcher starts getting too hot to touch.
Step 6:
Remove the pitcher from the steam wand then wipe and purge the steam wand.
Step 7:
Swirl and tap the pitcher to remove big air bubbles. Milk should look silky and smooth.
Step 8:
Pour your desired latte art.

You have now made a delicious and beautiful latte!

*Tips for Different Milks*
Type of Milk Aeration Time Temperature Rest Time
Whole Milk 3 seconds 160°F No rest
Oat Milk 5 seconds 160°F 5 seconds
Almond Milk 5 seconds 160°F 10 seconds
